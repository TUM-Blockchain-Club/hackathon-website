Visual Pattern Language

  • Boxy theme: every corner is square (90 degrees, 0 radius). The Sponsors & Partners grid (logo-cloud-2) is the shape baseline — hard borders, sharp edges, boxed cells. Cards, controls, buttons, chips, inputs, panels, and images all follow it. No pills, no rounded cards, no circular avatars.
  • Base pages are black or near-black with white or warm-white foreground typography.
  • Hero backgrounds use oversized, soft orbital ribbon shapes instead of tiny decorative dots or boxed hero cards. For the selected ASCII hero, keep the TBC ribbon field and do not use the hero-ascii-one UnicornStudio background.
  • The strongest energy comes from diagonal or circular sweeps: yellow and orange on one edge, red through the middle, purple on the far edge.
  • Thin contour lines can appear in amber, purple, or rust red at low opacity.
  • The logo treatment pairs three type moods: clean sans TUM, pixel/block BLOCKCHAIN, and red hand-drawn Hackathon.
  • The red Hackathon lettering has a black outline or shadow and should remain SVG artwork, not live text.

Usage Rules

  • Use black or very dark burgundy as the default page background.
  • Use white and off-white for primary readable copy; reserve pure white for logos and major headings.
  • Use the yellow-red-purple gradient for important actions, dividers, borders, and high-emphasis text only.
  • Use #F44336 for Hackathon-specific marks and short accents.
  • Keep orange and amber as edge light, not as a full background palette.
  • Avoid flat purple-gradient SaaS styling. The reference look is darker, warmer, and more atmospheric.
  • Avoid large white cards. If content needs framing, use transparent dark surfaces with #FFFFFF1A or #FFFFFF26 borders.
  • Prefer full-bleed hero imagery and ribbons with integrated logo placement over boxed hero cards.

Contrast Guidance

  • White on black, burgundy, and plum is safe for primary copy.
  • Gradient text should be used at large sizes only, with enough dark background behind it.
  • Red #F44336 on black is strong for display marks but should not be used for long body text.
  • Amber #FFC110 can be used for small accents, but avoid placing it over orange or red background areas.
  • Purple #6F3DE2 needs black or dark backing to stay legible.
